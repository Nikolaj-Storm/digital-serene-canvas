Neural Networks in Historical Text Analysis: A Computational Approach to Literary Evolution

2024 Digital Humanities Quarterly

Abstract

This paper presents a novel approach to analyzing historical texts using neural network architectures, demonstrating how computational methods can reveal patterns of literary evolution across centuries. By applying transformer-based models to a corpus of English literature spanning from the medieval period to the modern era, we identify previously unnoticed patterns in linguistic development, thematic evolution, and stylistic change. Our methodology combines traditional literary analysis with cutting-edge machine learning techniques, offering new insights into the development of English literary tradition.

Introduction

The application of neural networks to historical text analysis represents a significant advancement in digital humanities methodology. Traditional approaches to understanding literary evolution have relied primarily on close reading and limited comparative analysis. While these methods provide deep insights into individual works, they struggle to identify broad patterns across large corpora of texts spanning multiple centuries.

Our research addresses this limitation by employing state-of-the-art neural network architectures to analyze patterns in historical English literature. By training models on carefully curated datasets representing different historical periods, we can identify subtle shifts in language use, thematic development, and stylistic preferences that may not be apparent through traditional analytical methods.

Methodology

We constructed a comprehensive dataset comprising over 10,000 literary works from the 12th to the 21st centuries, representing major genres including poetry, drama, and prose fiction. The texts were preprocessed using natural language processing techniques and analyzed using a combination of BERT-based models and custom neural architectures designed specifically for historical text analysis.

Our approach combines unsupervised learning techniques for pattern discovery with supervised methods for validation against known historical linguistic changes. This hybrid methodology allows us to identify both expected evolutionary patterns and novel insights into literary development.

Key Findings

Our analysis reveals several significant patterns in the evolution of English literature. We observe clear evidence of linguistic stratification corresponding to major historical periods, with particularly notable transitions occurring during the Renaissance and Industrial Revolution periods. Additionally, our models identify previously unrecognized influences between authors separated by centuries, suggesting a more complex web of literary influence than previously understood.
